Active Components

Solid-State Active Devices

Active components can control current through electrical fields, or physical electron gating barriers. This is primarily done by manipulating materials with semi-conductor properties and bandgap physics. Esentially, we dope a layer of insulating material with a material with 5 valence electrons, so there is an extra electron after the octet rule. We then dope a layer of insulating material with a material with 3 valence electrons, so there is a missing electron after the octet rule. This inbalance in valence electrons causes electrons to freely flow to fill in the missing holes, which causes an electric field due to the charge imbalance. This inherently creates a bias for current to flow a singular direction.

The Semiconductor Lexicon

Diodes
Two-terminal devices that permit electrical current to flow uniquely in one forward bias directions.
Transistors
Three-terminal semiconductor device utilized for switching networks or continuous signal amplification control loops.
